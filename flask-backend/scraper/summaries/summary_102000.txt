Here's a summary of the Corporate Services Committee meeting for residents:

*   **Reviewed Flag Policy:** The committee directed staff to prepare new rules for displaying flags at City Hall. This will clarify what flags can be flown on city property in the future.
*   **Updated Building Illumination Rules:** New rules are being developed for how City of London buildings and amenities will be lit up with special colours. This will formalize the schedule for public light displays.
*   **Extended Pride Month Lighting Approved:** City buildings with multi-colour lights will now be illuminated in rainbow colours for the *entire remainder of June* to recognize National Pride Month.
*   **Special Illumination for Our London Family:** The new lighting policy confirms that City buildings will be illuminated in purple and green on June 6th to remember Our London Family.
*   **Illuminations for National Observances:** City buildings will also display specific colours for significant national days, including red and white for Canada Day (July 1) and orange for National Day for Truth and Reconciliation (September 30).
*   **Illuminations for Community Campaigns:** The policy includes lighting City buildings in red for Remembrance Day (November 10-11) and purple for the "Shine the Light on Woman Abuse" campaign during the remainder of November.
*   **Holiday Season Lighting:** City buildings will be illuminated in traditional red and green colours throughout December for the holiday season.
